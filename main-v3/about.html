About.

Two threads run through everything here. The first is research: what happens to institutions — regulators, labor markets, review systems — when their decisions start routing through AI. That work has one peer-reviewed acceptance, an Oxford Saïd research assistantship behind it, and a public failure log, all documented on the research site.

The second is operating. Nine conference secretariats, a logistics venture run as CFO, recruiting pipelines rebuilt with AI at two organizations — ₹17.9L of revenue managed across them. The operating work is where the research questions come from: every oversight-saturation model started as watching a real queue back up.

The rule connecting both: numbers you can check, failures kept on the record, systems that outlive the event they were built for.
