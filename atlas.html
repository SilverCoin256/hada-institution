The five metrics

One instrument per research question

Each metric operationalizes a specific paper in this program. None of them claim to fully identify institutional quality or causality on their own — the documentation is explicit about that. The dials are instrument schematics, not live readings.

QAI — Queue Acceleration Index

Backlog pressure

(Pendingt − Pendingt-1) / Completionst. A simple, auditable measure of whether an administrative queue is accelerating faster than it's being cleared.

Supports a planned submission — no paper yet
SEDI — State-Estimation Degradation Index

Oversight observability

Computable purely from boundary telemetry — timestamps, queue depth, dispute rates — without inspecting the content of the decisions under review.

→ Capacity Degradation in Distributed Human-in-the-Loop AI Pipelines
Authorization Saturation

Substantive vs. ceremonial review

A coupled-queue formalization of the point at which institutions substitute fast sign-off for slow, substantive review.

→ Modeling Authorization Saturation
Procedural Capacity

Review, contest, hold accountable

An institution's joint capacity to review an AI-influenced decision, let someone contest it, and hold someone accountable for it.

→ Simulating AI Governance Failure
ASI — Accountability Specificity Index

Rhetoric vs. binding authority

An 8-dimension coding scheme scoring how specifically a governance document assigns responsibility, versus merely invoking it.

→ Who Owns the Decision?
Public data

What's actually in it

Small pilot tables from public sources, not scraped-and-hoped-for big data.

EOIR / USCIS / CFPB / SEC pilot tables ~450 records CSV CC BY 4.0
ASI-adjudicated governance corpus 23 documents coded excerpts codebook original
Institution / queue-observation schema tables 5-entity model CSV + manifest checksummed

What this is not: a live dashboard, a leaderboard, or a claim of ground truth. Two candidate data sources (SSA and Meta institutional snapshots) were excluded from the first release after failing a retrieval audit. There is no public leaderboard yet — the benchmarks exist as regression tests and classroom exercises until independent labels and frozen splits are in place. See the notebook for the release notes.
